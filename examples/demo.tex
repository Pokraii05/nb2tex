\section{Theoretical overview}\label{theoretical-overview}

A real voltage source can be modeled as an ideal electromotive force
(emf) \(U_q\) in series with an internal resistance \(R_i\). The
terminal voltage \(U_k\) across the source depends on the current \(I\)
drawn by the load resistor \(R_L\).


\begin{equation}
U_k = U_q - I \, R_i
\label{eq:1}
\end{equation}

The short-circuit current \(I_k\) (when \(U_k = 0\)) is therefore:


\begin{equation}
I_k = \frac{U_q}{R_i}
\label{eq:2}
\end{equation}

When no current flows (\(I = 0\)), the terminal voltage equals the emf:


\begin{equation}
U_k = U_q
\label{eq:3}
\end{equation}

This linear relationship between \(U_k\) and \(I\) allows one to
determine both \(R_i\) and \(U_q\) by fitting a straight line to the
measured voltage-current data.
